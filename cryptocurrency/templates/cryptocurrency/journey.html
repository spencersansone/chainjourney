My Mining Journey


During August of 2017, there was a day when I was thinking to myself “what can I do to make some extra money on the side as a financial safety net?” At the time, I was working two jobs and had some extra money to invest. However, I didn’t really know what exactly to invest in. After doing much research, I found out that there was such a thing as cryptocurrency mining. For my senior project in high school, I had created my own gaming computer from parts and loved it; I figured that this would be similar to that project except I would dedicate this computer to mining only.

I searched through various YouTube videos and cryptocurrency forums in an attempt to get a clear understanding of how exactly this process would work. YouTube channels such as Voskcoin, IMineBlocks and Genesis Mining have given me many ideas and lessons that would further my interest in starting my own machines to get started. Many other cryptocurrency websites that discussed topics such as exchanges, pools and other general cryptocurrency information were also very helpful. What astonished me about this process was that a will to learn, persistence and a connection to the internet can be all that one needs to learn about how to start a cryptocurrency mining operation (or anything else for that matter.) This realization led me to the beginning of my journey

The first step in my journey was figuring out what components I needed in order to build a mining computer. This task is similar to building a regular desktop computer from scratch with a few modifications; these are the parts that I had ordered:

  • USB Flash Drive with at least 8gb (can use SSD/HDD as an alternative)
  • RAM: Crucial 4GB DDR4 (Single stick)
  • Motherboard: MSI Z170A SLI Plus
  • Processor: Intel Celeron G3900 Dual-core 2.8 GHz
  • Power Supply: Corsair RM1000x 1000W Gold Certified
  • PCIe Risers: MintCell 6-Pack
  • Video Card: GTX 1070 8GB GDDR5

I put all of the parts together and decided that it would be a good idea to install a solid state drive with Windows 10 as the operating system. After powering on the computer and updating the operating system and drivers, I was ready to start loading the essential programs. Firstly, I installed wallet software so that I could send and receive cryptocurrencies. I chose to use Jaxx since it enables the user to utilize many different cryptocurrencies. There are also many other wallets out there for everyone to try. (Check out my Sources page) Some cryptocurrency exchanges will give you a wallet address as well if you prefer to keep it there.

After setting up my wallet, I had a wallet address for Ethereum and was ready to start thinking about what I needed to do to get started with mining. I decided that I was interested in mining Ethereum with my video card and installed Nanopool’s Claymore miner. After the program was configured, I started the mining program and then left it alone to run 24 hours a day and 7 days a week.

After a month or two of my experience with mining Ethereum, I had noticed that there were many operating system crashes when using Windows 10. This became frustrating when I had come home from a long day many times to realize that my miner was not functional. Every second is valuable when mining and this was not an acceptable series of events. At this point, I decided that something needed to change; I switched to a new service I found called SimpleMining. This awesome service allows those that mine cryptocurrencies to track their machines’ statistics through a website by installing an image file to a flash drive and assigning an email address to a configuration file. The cost of using this service is $2 per machine per month and can be paid by cryptocurrency. Overall, I am very satisfied with this service because I have experienced far less crashing than I had before and can see live details quickly.
